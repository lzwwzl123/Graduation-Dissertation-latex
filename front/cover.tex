\begin{cabstract}

面向载人航天训练与复杂运动环境模拟需求，地面加速度模拟装备需要在较大工作空间内实现持续、平滑且高动态的加速度输出，以支持航天员在交会对接、姿态调整和长时间机动任务中的前庭适应与操控能力训练。传统离心机虽可提供较大径向过载，但存在设备体积大、运行成本高、运动形式单一等不足，难以模拟多姿态耦合条件下的复杂加速度环境。
绳驱并联机器人采用柔性绳索替代刚性连杆，具有工作空间大、动平台惯量低、负载自重比高和动态响应快等优点，为新型高动态加速度模拟平台的构建提供了可行方案。

本文采用平面四绳冗余驱动方案构建机器人总体机构，完成了末端平台、出绳方式、关键锚点布局及传感器配置的设计分析，并提出动态出绳点设计思路以简化机构。末端平台采用创新绕绳方法，实现连续多圈旋转，并结合张力传感器布置为后续张力闭环控制提供硬件基础。围绕控制需求，建立了绳长、平台位姿与驱动端状态之间的运动学关系，采用基于
雅可比矩阵迭代的正运动学方法实现末端位姿估计，并在样机平台上完成位置控制流程验证。

研究与仿真结果表明，基于逆运动学离散求解的常规位置控制虽能实现给定轨迹的基本跟随，但在轨迹离散、绳索柔性、摩擦及系统耦合等因素影响下，末端加速度易出现不连续和抖振，难以满足高动态平滑加速度模拟要求，同时由于模型精度与实际差异，极易发生绳索松脱。为此，本文将控制重点由位置控制转向力控制，围绕张力分配问题开展分析与优化。针对平面四绳冗余驱动机构中末端期望加速度对
应多组可行绳力解的特点，分析了正张力约束、力分布均衡性及峰值载荷限制对张力分配结果的影响，并结合轨迹生成过程对末端加速度和理论绳索力进行了仿真计算。

在此基础上，本文搭建了力控制实验平台，完成单电机闭环力矩控制、四电机协同张力控制以及末端平台加速度跟踪实验。综合理论分析、仿真研究与阶段性实验结果表明，所设计的高动态连续旋转平面绳驱并联机器人具备开展连续旋转与高动态控制研究的基础，采用力控制代替单纯位置控制是改善末端加速度连续性、提升动态响应品质的合理技术路线。
本文研究可为连续旋转绳驱并联机器人的设计与控制提供参考，并为面向航天训练、飞行模拟及其他高动态运动模拟场景的装备研制提供理论依据与工程借鉴。

\end{cabstract}

\begin{eabstract}

To meet the demands of manned spaceflight training and complex motion-environment simulation, ground-based acceleration simulators are expected to generate continuous, smooth, and high-dynamic acceleration output over a relatively large workspace, so as to support astronauts' vestibular adaptation and manipulation training during rendezvous and docking, attitude adjustment, and long-duration maneuvering tasks. Although traditional centrifuges can provide considerable radial overload, they suffer from large size, high operating cost, and limited motion patterns, making them unsuitable for reproducing complex acceleration environments under multi-attitude coupled motions. Cable-driven parallel robots, which use flexible cables instead of rigid links, possess the advantages of large workspace, low moving-platform inertia, high payload-to-weight ratio, and fast dynamic response, and therefore provide a feasible solution for a new type of high-dynamic acceleration simulation platform.

In this thesis, a planar four-cable redundantly actuated configuration is adopted to design the overall mechanism. The moving platform, cable-routing mode, key anchor-point layout, and sensor configuration are analyzed, and a dynamic cable-exit concept is proposed to simplify the mechanical design. An innovative cable-wrapping scheme is employed on the moving platform to realize continuous multi-turn rotation, and a tension-sensor arrangement is introduced to provide hardware support for subsequent closed-loop tension control. For control-oriented analysis, the kinematic relationships among cable lengths, platform pose, and actuator-side states are established. A forward kinematics method based on Jacobian iterative solution is employed for end-effector pose estimation, and the position-control procedure is verified on the prototype platform.

Theoretical analysis and simulation results show that conventional position control based on discretized inverse kinematics can achieve basic trajectory tracking. However, under the combined effects of trajectory discretization, cable flexibility, friction, and system coupling, the end-platform acceleration tends to exhibit discontinuity and chattering, which makes it difficult to satisfy the requirement of smooth high-dynamic acceleration simulation.Meanwhile, owing to the mismatch between the mathematical model and the actual system, cable slackening is likely to occur. Therefore, the control focus is shifted from position control to force control. The tension-distribution problem is analyzed and optimized for the planar four-cable redundantly actuated mechanism, in which one desired end-platform acceleration generally corresponds to multiple feasible tension solutions. The influences of positive-tension constraints, load-balancing requirements, and peak-load limitations on the tension-distribution results are investigated, and simulations of end-platform acceleration and theoretical cable tensions along the planned trajectories are carried out.

On this basis, a force-control experimental platform is developed, and experiments including single-motor closed-loop torque control, four-motor coordinated tension control, and end-platform acceleration tracking are conducted. The theoretical analysis, simulation results, and staged experimental validation indicate that the proposed high-dynamic continuously rotating planar cable-driven parallel robot provides a sound basis for continuous-rotation and high-dynamic control research. Replacing pure position control with force control is shown to be a reasonable technical route for improving acceleration continuity and dynamic performance. This work can provide useful references for the design and control of continuously rotating cable-driven parallel robots, and also offer theoretical and engineering support for equipment development in astronaut training, flight simulation, and other high-dynamic motion-simulation scenarios.

\end{eabstract}
