\begin{cabstract}

面向载人航天训练与复杂运动环境模拟需求，地面加速度模拟装备需要在较大工作空间内实现持续、高带宽且平滑的动态输出，以支持航天员在交会对接、姿态调整和长时间机动任务中的前庭适应与操控能力训练。传统离心机虽然能够提供较大的径向过载，但设备体积大、运行成本高，且运动形式相对单一，难以模拟多姿态耦合运动下的复杂加速度环境。绳驱并联机器人采用柔性绳索替代刚性连杆，具有工作空间大、动平台惯量低、负载自重比高和动态响应快等优点，为构建新型高动态加速度模拟平台提供了可行方案。

本文采用平面四绳冗余驱动方案构建机器人总体机构，完成了末端平台、出绳方式、关键锚点布局以及传感器配置的设计分析。针对大转动惯量和连续旋转工况下传统滑轮导向方案在结构复杂度和承载能力方面的不足，研究中采用动态出绳设计思路，补偿平面出绳滑轮包络绳索长度；末端平台采用创新绕绳方法，实现了连续多圈旋转的效果。并结合张力传感器布置方案，为后续张力闭环控制与实验验证提供硬件基础。围绕机构控制需求，建立了绳长、平台位姿与驱动端状态之间的运动学关系，采用基于雅可比矩阵迭代的正运动学求解方法实现末端位姿估计，并在样机平台上完成位置控制流程验证。研究与仿真表明，基于逆运动学离散求解的常规位置控制能够实现给定轨迹的基本跟随，但在轨迹参数离散、绳索柔性、摩擦以及系统耦合等因素共同作用下，末端加速度容易出现不连续和抖振现象，难以满足高动态平滑加速度模拟要求。因此，本文将控制重点由位置控制转向力控制，尝试通过张力直接调节末端广义力，实现更连续的加速度输出。

在控制算法方面，本文围绕张力分配问题开展了分析、仿真与优化。针对平面四绳冗余驱动机构中末端期望加速度对应多组可行绳力解的特点，分析了正张力约束、力分布均衡性以及峰值载荷控制等要求对张力分配结果的影响，并结合轨迹生成过程对末端加速度和理论绳索力进行了仿真计算，为后续控制器设计提供了依据。

综合理论分析、仿真研究与阶段性实验结果可以看出，所设计的高动态连续旋转平面绳驱并联机器人方案具备开展连续旋转与高动态控制研究的基础，采用力控制代替单纯位置控制是改善末端加速度连续性、提升动态响应品质的合理技术路线。本文完成的平面四绳机构设计、运动学求解、位置控制验证、张力分配分析、力控制实验平台搭建以及单电机闭环力矩控制、四电机协同张力控制、末端平台加速度跟踪实验。本文研究可为连续旋转绳驱并联机器人的设计与控制提供参考，并为面向航天训练、飞行模拟及其他高动态运动模拟场景的装备研制提供理论依据与工程借鉴。
\end{cabstract}

\begin{eabstract}

  嗷嗷嗷嗷嗷嗷嗷还得翻译
   Facing the requirements of manned spaceflight training and complex motion environment simulation, ground-based acceleration simulation equipment must achieve continuous, high-bandwidth, and smooth dynamic output within a large workspace to support astronauts’ vestibular adaptation and manipulative capability training during rendezvous and docking, attitude adjustment, and long-duration maneuvering missions. While traditional centrifuges can provide significant radial overload, they suffer from large equipment size, high operational costs, and relatively limited motion forms, making it difficult to simulate complex acceleration environments under multi-attitude coupled motions. Cable-driven parallel robots, which use flexible cables instead of rigid links, offer advantages such as a large workspace, low moving platform inertia, high payload-to-weight ratio, and fast dynamic response, providing a feasible solution for constructing a new type of high-dynamic acceleration simulation platform.

This paper adopts a planar four-cable redundantly actuated configuration to construct the overall robot mechanism, completing the design and analysis of the end-platform, cable exit methods, key anchor point layouts, and sensor configuration. To address the limitations of traditional pulley guiding methods in terms of structural complexity and load capacity under high rotational inertia and continuous rotation conditions, a dynamic cable exit design approach is employed. An innovative cable winding method is adopted for the end-platform, achieving continuous multi-turn rotation. Combined with the tension sensor layout scheme, this provides a hardware foundation for subsequent tension closed-loop control and experimental verification. Focusing on the control requirements of the mechanism, the kinematic relationships among cable length, platform pose, and driving-end states are established. A forward kinematics solution method based on Jacobian matrix iteration is used to estimate the end pose, and position control process verification is completed on the prototype platform. Research and simulations indicate that conventional position control based on discrete inverse kinematics solutions can achieve basic tracking of a given trajectory; however, under the combined effects of discrete trajectory parameters, cable flexibility, friction, and system coupling, the end acceleration is prone to discontinuities and chattering, making it difficult to meet the requirements for high-dynamic smooth acceleration simulation. Therefore, this paper shifts the control focus from position control to force control, attempting to achieve more continuous acceleration output by directly regulating the end generalized force through tension.

In terms of control algorithms, this paper conducts analysis, simulation, and optimization concerning the tension distribution problem. Addressing the characteristic that the desired end acceleration in the planar four-cable redundantly actuated mechanism corresponds to multiple feasible cable tension solutions, the influence of requirements such as positive tension constraints, force distribution balance, and peak load control on the tension distribution results is analyzed. Simulations of the end acceleration and theoretical cable forces are also carried out in conjunction with the trajectory generation process, providing a basis for subsequent controller design.

Synthesizing the theoretical analysis, simulation research, and preliminary experimental results, it can be seen that the designed high-dynamic continuous-rotation planar cable-driven parallel robot scheme possesses the foundation for conducting research on continuous rotation and high-dynamic control. Adopting force control instead of pure position control is a reasonable technical approach to improving end acceleration continuity and enhancing dynamic response quality. The work completed in this paper includes the design of the planar four-cable mechanism, kinematic solution, position control verification, tension distribution analysis, force control experimental platform setup, as well as single-motor closed-loop torque control, four-motor cooperative tension control, and end-platform acceleration tracking experiments. The research in this paper can provide a reference for the design and control of continuously rotating cable-driven parallel robots and offer a theoretical basis and engineering reference for the development of equipment for aerospace training, flight simulation, and other high-dynamic motion simulation scenarios.
\end{eabstract}
